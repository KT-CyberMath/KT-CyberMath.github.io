Dataset and Distribution

A dataset is a structured collection of data, usually organized in tables containing rows (observations) and columns (variables). Each column represents a measurable attribute, while each row represents a single entity, such as a person, event, or object.

A distribution describes how the values of a variable are spread across the dataset. It shows the frequency or probability of different outcomes and helps visualize patterns, clusters, and outliers. Distributions can be univariate (analyzing one variable at a time) or multivariate (analyzing relationships among two or more variables).

Caesar Cipher on Greek Text

To illustrate a simple encryption, the Caesar cipher with shift +3 is applied to the poem’s opening line.

Original:
Σα βγεις στον πηγαιμό για την Ιθάκη

Encrypted (+3 shift):
φυ εδλζ συξξ πκεδεξξ δκε τοδ Κκδνν

Decoded (−3 shift):
Σα βγεις στον πηγαιμό για την Ιθάκη

The Caesar cipher is a basic substitution cipher. When applied to languages with limited alphabets, statistical properties such as letter distributions can reveal the shift used, connecting cryptography and descriptive statistics.
