1) Concepts: Dataset & Distribution

A dataset is a collection of observations described by variables. A distribution tells how values of a variable are spread. Distributions can be univariate (one variable at a time) or multivariate (two or more variables jointly). Univariate views (histogram, bar chart, percent table) are quick for a single feature; multivariate views (scatter plots, contingency tables, correlation) show relationships between variables.

Tip. Start univariate to understand each variable’s range, center, and spread. Then, if you suspect dependence, switch to multivariate tools (scatter plot for numeric–numeric, grouped bars for categorical–categorical, faceting for mixed types).

3) RSA “toy” encryption for letters (per prof. notes)

We map A→0, B→1, …, Z→25 and encode each letter as a number. Choose small primes p=29, q=31 → N=pq=899 and ϕ(N)=(29−1)(31−1)=840. Pick public exponent e=17 (coprime with 840). The private exponent d is the modular inverse of 17 mod 840, so d=593 (since 17×593 ≡ 1 (mod 840)).

Prof’s algebra (encoding C = Me mod N, decoding M = Cd mod N) follows from Euler’s theorem: Mϕ(N) ≡ 1 (mod N) when M and N are coprime, and from the identity e·d = 1 + k·ϕ(N).

Important. Frequency analysis that breaks a Caesar cipher does not help with RSA, because the mapping is not a simple shift on 0–25 anymore. For this toy exercise we show the math and use the known key pair (e,d) to decrypt.

4) Greek text choice: Καβάφης — «Ιθάκη»

A short tribute: Constantine P. Cavafy (Κωνσταντίνος Π. Καβάφης) is a major Greek poet whose lucid, reflective style shaped modern Greek literature. «Ιθάκη» was a favorite growing up. Below we show the poem and the empirical letter distribution (Greek alphabet).
