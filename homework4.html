1) Introduction

This page presents a concise simulation of the Law of Large Numbers (LLN). We show that for Bernoulli trials with success probability $p$, the relative frequency $f(n)$ of successes after $n$ trials converges towards $p$ as $n$ increases.

2) Concept

Let $X_1, X_2, \\ldots, X_n$ be i.i.d. Bernoulli$(p)$ variables. The sample mean $\\bar X_n = \\tfrac{1}{n}\\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_i$ is the relative frequency of success after $n$ trials. The LLN states that $\\bar X_n \\to p$ as $n \\to \\infty$ (in probability, and almost surely).

What the professor expects: multiple independent trajectories of $f(n)$ vs $n$, plus a histogram showing the distribution of final $f(n)$ values across trajectories.

3) Simulation design

  • Model: Bernoulli$(p)$, with $p = 0.5$ (coin) — adjustable.
  • Trials per trajectory: $n \\in \\{10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, 1000\\}$.
  • Number of trajectories: $m = 12$.
  • Outputs: a table of selected $f(n)$ values, a comment on convergence, and a histogram description for the final $f(n)$ across $m$ trajectories.

Pseudocode (language-agnostic)

set p = 0.5
set N = [10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, 1000]
set m = 12
for each j in 1..m:
    successes = 0
    for each n in N:
        repeat until we reach n: successes += Bernoulli(p)
        f[n, j] = successes / n
record f[n, j] for report & plots

5) Interpretation

Each individual path may oscillate early, but as $n$ increases the fluctuations diminish and all paths cluster near $p$. The histogram of final $f(n)$ values tightens around $p$, confirming that empirical frequencies approximate the true probability when the sample size grows.
