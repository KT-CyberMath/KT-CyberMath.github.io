1) Introduction

This homework demonstrates the Law of Large Numbers (LLN), a cornerstone of probability theory. It states that as the number of trials increases, the empirical mean of random outcomes approaches the theoretical expectation.

2) Concept

For independent random variables \(X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n\) with expected value \(E[X]\), the sample mean is: 

f(n) = (X₁ + X₂ + … + Xₙ) / n

According to the LLN: limₙ→∞ f(n) = E[X]. In other words, as we increase the number of observations, the average of the results tends to stabilize around the true probability.

Example: Tossing a biased coin with probability p = 0.6 of heads. As the number of tosses grows, the proportion of heads converges to 0.6.

The LLN confirms that randomness smooths out with repetition. In statistics and cybersecurity analytics, this principle ensures that averages based on large datasets yield reliable estimates of true probabilities.
