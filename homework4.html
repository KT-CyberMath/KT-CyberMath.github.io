1) Introduction

This homework demonstrates the Law of Large Numbers (LLN), one of the most fundamental results in probability. It states that as the number of trials increases, the empirical mean of repeated random outcomes approaches the theoretical expectation.

2) Concept

For independent random variables X₁, X₂, …, Xₙ with expected value E[X], the sample mean is: 

f(n) = (X₁ + X₂ + … + Xₙ) / n

According to the LLN: limₙ→∞ f(n) = E[X]. As n increases, the average of the observations tends to stabilize around the true probability.

Example: Tossing a biased coin with probability p = 0.6 of heads. As the number of tosses grows, the proportion of heads converges to 0.6.

6) Conclusion

The Law of Large Numbers confirms that randomness smooths out with repetition. It forms the theoretical foundation of statistical inference, ensuring that averages from large samples are reliable estimates of true probabilities.
