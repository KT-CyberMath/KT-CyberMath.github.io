Homework 8 — Bernoulli Process, Binomial & Combinatorics

Konstantinos Tziakouris • Matricola 2229757 • Statistics • A.Y. 2025–2026

1) Analogy with Homework 7

In HW7, each week was a Bernoulli trial: “secure” vs “breach”. Let π be the breach probability per week. Over n weeks, the number of breaches K follows Binomial(n, π). The random-walk total score was Sn = n − 2K. Thus HW7’s simulation is an empirical view of the same Bernoulli process formalized here.

2) Binomial Coefficients & Counting Trajectories

The probability of exactly k breaches is P(K=k) = C(n,k) πk (1−π)n−k, where C(n,k) = n! / (k!(n−k)!) counts how many sequences contain k breaches and n−k secure weeks. Each such sequence maps to a unique random-walk path with final score Sn = n − 2k.

3) Pascal’s Triangle & Binomial Expansion

The rows of Pascal’s triangle list the coefficients C(n,0), C(n,1), …, C(n,n). They are the coefficients in the binomial expansion (p + q)^n = Σ C(n,k) p^k q^{n-k}. Setting p = π and q = 1−π yields the complete Bernoulli law of outcomes.

4) Law of Large Numbers (LLN) & Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

  • LLN: The empirical breach proportion K/n converges to π as n → ∞.
  • Variance: Var(K) = n π (1−π).
  • CLT (approximation): For large n, K is approximately normal with mean nπ and variance nπ(1−π), hence Sn = n − 2K is also approximately normal after an affine transform.

5) Fibonacci & Combinatorial Relationships (brief)

With constraints on paths (e.g., forbid going below zero, or forbid consecutive breaches), the number of admissible trajectories follows simple recurrences. For example, the number of binary strings of length n without consecutive 1’s equals F_{n+2}, where F_k are Fibonacci numbers. These are not part of HW7 but show how combinatorial coefficients pattern subsets of walks.

6) Similarities & Differences (HW7 vs HW8)

  • Similarities: same Bernoulli mechanism; same binomial counting; same final-score mapping Sn=n−2K.
  • Differences: HW7 is simulation-first; HW8 formalizes counting via C(n,k), connects to Pascal’s triangle and binomial expansion, and states LLN/CLT.
